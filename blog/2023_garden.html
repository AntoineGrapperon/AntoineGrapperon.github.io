# Gardening notes for the 2023 season

In May 2023, I was granted the use of a 14 m2 lot dedicated to gardening. While my family has quite a few talented gardener, this is a first for me. The first mistake I made was not to keep detailed notes of what I planted and when. Without notes, I am doomed to repeat more or less the same mistakes next year. In the notes below I am trying to reconstruct my memories.

# 2023-05-01

  • I received a plot covered with weeds. Used forks and grelinette to turn the soil and root out the weeds. It took around 4 hours.
  • What I should have done: turn the soil on day one, let to dry for a few days, only then remove the weeds, it will be easier to shake off the soil.
  • There were a few plants remaining from previous tenants: I could not figure it at the time: onions and egyptian garlic

# 2023-05-15

  • I added around 300L of shrimp compost and spread it on all the surface.

# 2023-05-20

  • I purchased seeds: radish (round ones), carrots, peas, green beans, spinach, green letuce
  • I purchase tomato plants: 6 different varietis of tomatoes (pink lady (big tomatoes), honey tomatores, grape tomatoes - 2 types, black tomatoes).
  • I planted the tomatoes "slanted" instead of vertical because I was advised it would help the plant my more roots. I did not cut enough branches as the plant was growing up (especially at the bottom of the plant), later the branches grew several meters long, rempant on the ground and starting fighting with other vegetables around. I used round cages as tutors for the plant but I could not anochor them deep enough in the ground, they ended up twisting and not resisting the weight of tomatoes.
  • I planted the seeds according to instructions on the seed bags.

# 2023-06-01

  • I am continuously removing weeds as they grow. But the soild is drying up and I have to water quite often.
  • Coriander is growing up on its own from seeds from last year I believe.
  • I invited a fried to come take care of the garden with me and plant her own stuff in there. She planted cucumber ( 1 plant), basil (3 plants), kale (3 plants), parsley (1 plant), thym (1 plant), strawberries (5 plants)
  • Neighbours gardeners gave me 2 plants of spicy red peppeter + 1 plant of mild green pepper.

# 2023-06-15

  • First radish harvest. They are good, very spicy. They don t conserve well on a plate in the frigde.
  • First lettuces, they are good.
  • First harvest of spinach leaves.
