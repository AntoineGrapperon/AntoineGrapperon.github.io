# Reducing reliance on car requires more than just an app

Today, I had the chance to be an attendee as Elodie Deschaintres was defending her PhD thesis in front of a jury. A thesis defense comes after 3 to 5 years of dedicated research work and it is the last obstacle before being called a Doctor. As you can imagine, it is a stressful experience!

The concept of "New Mobility" is often presented as our best hope to solve the transportation puzzle, to reduce congestion, emissions, travel times, while increasing equity. But the concept covers many new types of mobility, including shared mobility services (bike sharing, car sharing etc), car pooling, demand responsive transport, digital platform to integrate existing offering, autonomous vehicles, eletrical vehicles, intermodal trips. How will all these new modes of transport interact with each other? Can they really all be beneficial?

The "Old Mobility" (as opposed to the "New Mobility") is well known: it is supported by cars, transit, bikes and the good old shoes. We know under which conditions these modes are in competition and when they complement each other. But we do not have that knowledge when it comes to New Mobility! Elodie's thesis brings some light on this discussion. When her thesis becomes public, I will add a link to the publication.

Elodie's thesis was (approximately - because I am translating from French): Modelling interactions between transport modes by integrating various datasets.. She worked with all kinds of data (travel survey, traffic count, taxi data, bike sharing data etc) for the city of Montreal to draw a picture of how the different modes of transportations interact with each other. As cities around the world are aiming to reduce car ownership and to increase occupancy for motorized vehicles, it is important to check that we are walking in the right direction! When a city offers a new car-sharing program, is it actually helping? Elodie gave a thought provoking example during the Q&A session, which highlighted how we can become obsessed with technological solutions and loose sight the actual goal. I wish I had an audio recorder but you will have to make do with my paraphrase below!

It is not because we offer an app to pay for every mode of transport that we have alignment of all the actors: we still have to unify the fare system, make sure the different organizations are not incentivized to compete with each other.

-- Elodie Deschaintres heavily paraphrased by myself
