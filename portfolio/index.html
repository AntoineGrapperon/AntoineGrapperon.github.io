      # Portfolio

      This portfolio gathers some my proudest contributions to the EMME product. I try to be short and defer the reader to the official communications for further details (when possible).

      # EMME Flow

      A major advantage of the EMME software is its extensive Python APIs which allows users to develop and automate their transport model from creation to reporting. However, a significant part of its userbase does not come from a software engineering background and need something easier and more intuitive to work with than Python APIs.

      As a product manager, I led the discovery efforts to surface and understand the problem at hands using support tickets analysis and user-interviews. I confirmed value for a code-free automation platform by finding existing users who wanted to be Early Adopters and share their feedback. Internal stakeholders were involved very early in the discover and contributing which helped make the case. I assembled a team to help me build EMME Flow, communicated the product vision and priorities. I also led the delivery with weekly touchpoints with relevant stakeholders to go over design reviews, dev updates, testing framework, documentation and integration with EMME.

      Flow met a frank success with a high adoption rate among tracked users within the first year. Many EMME Flow users reached out to us (unprompted!) to share their delight and, as customary, to suggest improvements.

      Read more about EMME Flow - Code-free automation in the corresponding release announcement (opens new window)!

      # EMME Scenes

      A by-product of transport planning models is a wealth of synthetic data which can be used to validate/debug the model or to analyse its results and communicat a story. We wanted to provide EMME users with an easy way to do that directly from the software.

      I was part of the product team and I was the main Python and Vue.js developer. We built Scenes, a 3D, interactive and animated mapping framework. Each scene acted as a canned application to make it easy for the user to generate the analyses and render the video they needed. We launched with 3 scenes: the Network scene to make sense of traffic and transit assignment results, the Demand and Accessibility scene to show animated desire lines and the SBTA scene which focused on providing a Q&A application for transit assignment results.

      With great pleasure, we witnessed videos produced with Scenes starting to make their way into presentations during international conferences or on LinkedIn posts as EMME users started to adopt it.

      Read more about EMME Scenes in the corresponding announcement post (opens new window)!
